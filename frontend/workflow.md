
### **Automobile Repair Company Workflow**

This workflow details the responsibilities and handoffs between the Front Desk, Service Coordinator, Parts (Stores), Mechanics, and Accountants. The central document guiding this process is the **Repair Order (RO)**.

---

### Phase 1: Customer Intake & Diagnosis

**Objective:** Gather information, identify the problem, and create the Repair Order (RO).

1.  **Booking & Arrival (🚗 Front Desk Supervisor)**
    * Schedules appointments or handles walk-ins.
    * Greets the customer, confirms details (VIN, mileage), and records the customer's reported issue.
    * Creates a new **Repair Order (RO)** in the system.
    * Assigns the RO to the **Service Coordinator**.

2.  **Initial Triage (👨‍🔧 Service Coordinator)**
    * Receives the RO.
    * May perform a brief visual inspection or test drive with the customer to clarify the complaint.
    * Acts as the primary technical liaison between the customer and the mechanic.

3.  **Diagnosis (🔧 Mechanic)**
    * The **Service Coordinator** assigns the vehicle and RO to a **Mechanic**.
    * The **Mechanic** performs diagnostic tests to identify the root cause.
    * The **Mechanic** lists required repairs, estimated labor time, and a preliminary parts list on the RO.
    * The RO is returned to the **Service Coordinator**.

---

### Phase 2: Quotation & Customer Approval

**Objective:** Create an accurate cost estimate and get customer authorization.

1.  **Parts Sourcing (📦 Parts / Stores)**
    * The **Service Coordinator** sends the preliminary parts list to the **Parts** department.
    * **Parts** checks inventory, confirms pricing, and sources costs for any non-stocked items.
    * **Parts** provides a finalized parts list with costs back to the Service Coordinator.

2.  **Estimate Creation (👨‍🔧 Service Coordinator)**
    * The **Service Coordinator** compiles the full quotation by combining:
        * **Parts Cost**
        * **Labor Cost** (Estimated time x shop rate)
        * **Shop Fees/Taxes**
    * This detailed estimate is attached to the RO.

3.  **Customer Approval (📞 Front Desk Supervisor)**
    * The **Front Desk** (or Service Coordinator) contacts the customer to explain the repairs and present the total cost.
    * **Crucial Step:** Customer approval (or decline) is received and **documented on the RO**.

---

### Phase 3: Repair Execution

**Objective:** Complete the authorized work efficiently and accurately.

1.  **Job Scheduling (👨‍🔧 Service Coordinator)**
    * With approval, the **Service Coordinator** officially schedules the repair, assigns it back to a **Mechanic**, and notifies the **Parts** department.

2.  **Parts Issuing (📦 Parts / Stores)**
    * The **Mechanic** submits a formal parts request (based on the RO) to **Parts**.
    * **Parts** issues the items from inventory to that specific RO.
    * **Key Inventory Step:** This action updates the inventory system and allocates the parts' cost directly to the RO for billing.

3.  **The Repair (🔧 Mechanic)**
    * The **Mechanic** performs all repairs as listed on the approved RO.
    * **If new problems are found:** The Mechanic **STOPS** and notifies the **Service Coordinator** to get new customer approval (repeating Phase 2).
    * The **Mechanic** accurately logs all labor time spent.

---

### Phase 4: Quality Control & Billing

**Objective:** Ensure the work is done correctly and finances are recorded.

1.  **Final Inspection (👨‍🔧 Service Coordinator)**
    * The **Mechanic** signs off on the RO.
    * The **Service Coordinator** (or a lead mechanic) performs a **Quality Control (QC)** check (e.g., test drive, visual inspection).
    * The **Service Coordinator** "closes" the RO, verifying all parts and labor are correctly documented.

2.  **Invoicing (💰 Accountants)**
    * The finalized RO is sent to the **Accountants**.
    * The **Accountant** reviews the RO and generates the **final customer invoice**.
    * The **Accountant** posts the transaction (costs to "Cost of Goods Sold," invoice total to "Accounts Receivable").

3.  **Payment Prep (🚗 Front Desk Supervisor)**
    * The **Accountant** sends the finalized invoice to the **Front Desk**.
    * The **Front Desk** prepares for customer pickup and may contact the customer.

---

### Phase 5: Vehicle Handover & Post-Service

**Objective:** Finalize the customer interaction and close the books.

1.  **Customer Pickup (🚗 Front Desk Supervisor)**
    * The customer arrives. The **Front Desk** reviews the invoice with them and answers questions.
    * The **Front Desk** collects and **processes the payment**.

2.  **Final Reconciliation (💰 Accountants)**
    * The **Front Desk** sends the payment record to the **Accountants**.
    * The **Accountant** reconciles the payment, closing the invoice and moving the amount from "Accounts Receivable" to "Cash."

3.  **Ongoing Activities (All Departments)**
    * **Parts:** Manages inventory levels based on parts used and reorders stock.
    * **Accountants:** Manage payroll, pay suppliers (for parts), and generate profit/loss reports.
    * **Front Desk:** May perform a follow-up call to ensure customer satisfaction.
